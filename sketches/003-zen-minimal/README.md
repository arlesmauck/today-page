## Variant: Zen Minimal

### Design stance
Only what matters most, and nothing else. A single screen shows your greeting, the time, today's temperature, one top headline, and your next calendar event. Everything else is one click away. Designed to answer "what do I need to know right now?" and then stop.

### Key choices
- **Layout:** Ultra-centered, single column, massive whitespace. Vertically centered on load. Expands downward when you ask for more.
- **Typography:** Newsreader serif for editorial moments, Inter sans for structure. Giant, ghost-gray clock as ambient art.
- **Color:** Near-white background (#fefefe) with soft grays. No harsh borders. Almost nothing to look at.
- **Interaction:** "See everything" button expands the full dashboard below. Task checkboxes. Hover micro-interactions on stories.

### Trade-offs
- **Strong at:** Absolutely zero overwhelm. You literally cannot doom-scroll because there's almost nothing to scroll.
- **Weak at:** Requires an extra click to see "everything." Some users might find it *too* sparse.

### Best for
- People who want to break the compulsive refresh habit.
- The default view is a palette cleanser. The full view is there when you need it.
